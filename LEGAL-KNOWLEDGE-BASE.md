## 1. Germany — Practice License & Scope

### Heilpraktikergesetz (HeilPrG)

**Full title:** Gesetz über die berufsmäßige Ausübung der Heilkunde ohne Bestallung (Heilpraktikergesetz)  
**Date:** 17 February 1939 (last amended 2016)  
**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/heilprg/](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/heilprg/)

**Key provisions:**

- **§ 1 Abs. 1 HeilPrG:** "Wer die Heilkunde, ohne als Arzt bestallt zu sein, ausüben will, bedarf dazu der Erlaubnis." — Anyone wishing to practice healing arts without being a licensed physician requires a permit.
- **§ 1 Abs. 2 HeilPrG:** Defines "Ausübung der Heilkunde" as any professionally conducted activity in diagnosing, treating, or alleviating diseases, suffering, or bodily harm in humans.
- The HP-Psych (Heilpraktiker für Psychotherapie) is a restricted permit limited to psychotherapeutic methods. Holder may NOT prescribe medication, perform physical examinations, or treat somatic conditions.

**Erste Durchführungsverordnung zum HeilPrG (1. DVO-HeilPrG)**  
**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/heilprgdv_1/](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/heilprgdv_1/)

- **§ 2:** Permit is granted by the lower administrative authority (untere Verwaltungsbehörde) — in Munich, this is the Gesundheitsamt München (Referat für Gesundheit und Umwelt).
- **§ 2 Abs. 1 lit. i:** Permit may be denied if the applicant poses a danger to public health. The Überprüfung (examination) tests for this.

### Psychotherapeutengesetz (PsychThG)

**Full title:** Gesetz über den Beruf der Psychotherapeutin und des Psychotherapeuten (Psychotherapeutengesetz)  
**Date:** 15 November 2019 (reformed; original 1998)  
**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/psychthg_2020/](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/psychthg_2020/)

- **Relevance:** The reformed PsychThG created a new "Approbation" pathway (university degree → state exam → Approbation → Weiterbildung → Kassenzulassung). This does NOT affect existing HP-Psych permit holders.
- **§ 1 Abs. 1 PsychThG:** Defines "Psychotherapie" as treatment using scientifically recognized psychotherapeutic methods.
- **Title protection:** "Psychotherapeut/in" is now a protected title requiring Approbation. HP-Psych holders should NOT use this title — use "Heilpraktiker für Psychotherapie" or "Psychologischer Berater."
- **Coexistence:** HP-Psych permits remain valid. The two systems (Approbation vs. HP) coexist. HP-Psych holders may continue to practice psychotherapy within their permit scope.

### Scope of Practice — What HP-Psych Can and Cannot Do

| Allowed | Not Allowed |
|---------|-------------|
| Psychotherapy (talk therapy, psychoanalysis) | Prescribe medication |
| Psychodiagnostics (non-medical) | Medical diagnoses (ICD coding for billing is acceptable) |
| Psychologische Beratung | Physical examinations |
| Supervision of other practitioners | Use title "Psychotherapeut/in" (post-2019 PsychThG) |
| Online sessions (within Fernbehandlung rules) | Treat children under 3 without parental consent |
| Issue GebüH invoices | Bill public health insurance (GKV) |
| Accept private pay and PKV patients | Compulsory hospitalization |

### Berufsordnung (Professional Code)

- HP-Psych holders are NOT bound by the Muster-Berufsordnung für Psychologische Psychotherapeuten (this applies to approbierte Psychotherapeuten only).
- However, they ARE bound by:
  - General professional conduct standards (Sorgfaltspflicht)
  - § 203 StGB (confidentiality/professional secrecy — Schweigepflicht)
  - BGB contract law (Dienstvertrag, §§ 611 ff. BGB)

### § 203 StGB — Professional Secrecy

**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/stgb/__203.html](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/stgb/__203.html)

- **§ 203 Abs. 1 Nr. 1 StGB:** Covers Ärzte, Zahnärzte, Apotheker, and other healing professions.
- **Applicability to HP-Psych:** Debated. Majority legal opinion: HP-Psych holders ARE covered by § 203 StGB as persons "in a profession for which a state-regulated education is required" (Nr. 1) or as "Berater" (Nr. 6). Treat as binding.
- **Consequence:** Breach of confidentiality is a criminal offense (up to 1 year imprisonment or fine).

## 2. Germany — Data Protection (DSGVO)

### DSGVO / EU GDPR

**Full title:** Verordnung (EU) 2016/679 (Datenschutz-Grundverordnung)  
**Source:** [https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj)  
**German text:** [https://dsgvo-gesetz.de/](https://dsgvo-gesetz.de/)

### Key Articles for Health Data Processing

| Article | Topic | Relevance |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| **Art. 4(15)** | Definition of "health data" | Includes data about physical or mental health, provision of healthcare services |
| **Art. 6(1)(a)** | Consent | Legal basis for cookie/analytics processing |
| **Art. 6(1)(b)** | Contract performance | Legal basis for booking, payment, e-signature processing |
| **Art. 9(1)** | Special categories (health data) | General prohibition on processing health data |
| **Art. 9(2)(a)** | Explicit consent exception | Health data processing allowed with explicit consent |
| **Art. 9(2)(h)** | Health care exception | Processing for healthcare purposes by bound professional |
| **Art. 13** | Information obligation (direct collection) | Must provide privacy notice at point of data collection |
| **Art. 14** | Information obligation (indirect collection) | Must inform when data obtained from third parties |
| **Art. 15–22** | Data subject rights | Access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection |
| **Art. 28** | Data processor requirements | AVV (Auftragsverarbeitungsvertrag) required with all processors |
| **Art. 32** | Security of processing | Appropriate technical and organizational measures |
| **Art. 44–49** | International data transfers | Adequacy decisions, SCCs, BCRs for third-country transfers |

### Art. 9 — Health Data in Psychotherapy

- **Art. 9(2)(a):** Explicit consent. The client's informed consent to treatment implicitly covers necessary data processing, but DSGVO requires separate, specific, informed consent for data processing. The Datenschutz-Vereinbarung in the intake documents covers this.
- **Art. 9(2)(h):** Processing for healthcare by a professional bound by secrecy (§ 203 StGB). This is the primary legal basis for processing session-related health data.
- **Art. 9(3):** Member states may impose additional conditions on health data processing. Germany does via BDSG.

### BDSG (Bundesdatenschutzgesetz)

**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bdsg_2018/](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bdsg_2018/)

- **§ 22 BDSG:** Supplements Art. 9 DSGVO for German-specific health data processing conditions.
- **§ 22 Abs. 1 Nr. 1 lit. b BDSG:** Health data processing is lawful for healthcare purposes if processed by a person bound by professional secrecy (Schweigepflicht).
- **§ 22 Abs. 2 BDSG:** Requires "appropriate and specific measures" to safeguard data subjects (encryption, access controls, pseudonymization where possible).

### Practical Requirements

1. **Privacy notice** (Datenschutzerklärung) — Art. 13 DSGVO: Must disclose all processors, legal bases, retention periods, rights.
2. **AVV** (Auftragsverarbeitungsvertrag) — Art. 28 DSGVO: Required with every data processor:
   - Vercel (hosting)
   - Cal.com (booking)
   - Stripe (payment)
   - DocuSign or OpenAPI (e-signatures)
   - Google (Analytics, Ads — if consent given)
   - Microsoft (Clarity — if consent given)
3. **DSFA** (Datenschutz-Folgenabschätzung / DPIA) — Art. 35 DSGVO: May be required for systematic health data processing. Single practitioner with limited client volume is likely below threshold, but consider proactively.
4. **Verzeichnis der Verarbeitungstätigkeiten** (Records of processing activities) — Art. 30 DSGVO: Required for all controllers processing special category data. Must document each processing activity.
5. **Cookie consent** — ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC) as implemented in Germany via TTDSG § 25: Requires informed consent before setting non-essential cookies. Current implementation on the site is correct.

### Supervisory Authority

**Bayerisches Landesamt für Datenschutzaufsicht (BayLDA)**  
Promenade 18, 91522 Ansbach  
[https://www.lda.bayern.de/](https://www.lda.bayern.de/)  
Competent authority for non-public bodies in Bavaria.

### DSGVO Penalties (Art. 83)

| Tier | Maximum Fine | Violations |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Lower | €10M or 2% annual turnover | Records, security, DPIA, processor agreements |
| Upper | €20M or 4% annual turnover | Lawfulness, consent, data subject rights, international transfers |

**Realistic risk for solo practice:** First offense with no data breach → warning or fine in low thousands. Major breach with health data → potentially €5,000–50,000. Intentional violations or repeated non-compliance → higher.

### Google Fonts — LG München I Decision

**Court:** Landgericht München I  
**Case:** Az. 3 O 17493/20 (20 January 2022)  
**Ruling:** Loading Google Fonts from Google's CDN (fonts.googleapis.com) without consent transmits the user's IP address to Google, violating Art. 6(1)(f) DSGVO. Court awarded €100 damages.  
**Relevance:** The current site loads Google Fonts externally. Must either self-host or disclose + obtain consent.  
**Source:** [https://rewis.io/urteile/urteil/lhm-20-01-2022-3-o-1749320/](https://rewis.io/urteile/urteil/lhm-20-01-2022-3-o-1749320/)

## 3. Germany — Tax & VAT

### § 4 Nr. 14 UStG — VAT Exemption for Healthcare

**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/ustg_1980/__4.html](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/ustg_1980/__4.html)

- **§ 4 Nr. 14 lit. a UStG:** Exempts from VAT: "Heilbehandlungen im Bereich der Humanmedizin, die im Rahmen der Ausübung der Tätigkeit als Arzt, Zahnarzt, Heilpraktiker, Physiotherapeut, Hebamme oder einer ähnlichen heilberuflichen Tätigkeit durchgeführt werden."
- **Application:** Psychotherapy by HP-Psych is explicitly covered. Sessions are VAT-exempt.
- **Limitation:** Only therapeutic services are exempt. If offering pure coaching, consulting, or teaching (non-therapeutic), VAT may apply at 19%.

### Income Tax

- Freiberufler (freelance) status under § 18 EStG if meeting criteria.
- HP-Psych typically qualifies as Freiberufler → no Gewerbesteuer (trade tax), only Einkommensteuer.
- **§ 18 Abs. 1 Nr. 1 EStG:** Includes "heilkundliche Tätigkeit" as a Freiberuf.
- **Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/estg/__18.html](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/estg/__18.html)

### GebüH — Billing Basis

**Full title:** Gebührenordnung für Heilpraktiker (GebüH)  
**Relevance:** Standard fee schedule used for invoicing private insurance and Zusatzversicherungen. Not legally binding (unlike GOÄ for physicians) but widely recognized by insurers.  
**Typical codes:** Psychotherapeutic session (Ziff. 19.1–19.5), diagnostic interview (Ziff. 1.1).

## 4. Germany — Telehealth & Video

### Fernbehandlung (Remote Treatment)

- **Historical prohibition:** § 9 MBO-Ä (Musterberufsordnung für Ärzte) historically prohibited exclusive remote treatment. This was relaxed at the 121. Deutscher Ärztetag (2018).
- **HP-Psych:** Not bound by MBO-Ä (applies to physicians only). No legal prohibition on providing psychotherapy exclusively online.
- **Best practice:** Obtain informed consent for telehealth (covered by the Telehealth-Einwilligung in intake documents).

### Video Platform Requirements (DSGVO)

For psychotherapy sessions, the video platform must:
1. Be DSGVO-compliant (Art. 28 AVV available)
2. Offer end-to-end encryption (Art. 32 DSGVO — appropriate security)
3. Not store or access session content
4. Preferably be KBV-certified (for credibility, not legally required for HP-Psych)

**RED Medical (RED connect plus):**
- KBV-certified (Kassenärztliche Bundesvereinigung)
- Peer-to-peer, end-to-end encrypted
- No recording, no third-party access
- DSGVO-compliant, German company
- Works internationally (no IP restrictions)

## 5. Germany — Consumer Protection & Advertising

### Heilmittelwerbegesetz (HWG)

**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/heilmwerbg/](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/heilmwerbg/)

- **§ 3 HWG:** Prohibits misleading advertising for therapeutic services.
- **§ 11 HWG:** Restrictions on advertising methods (no before/after, no patient testimonials for Rx treatments — less relevant for psychotherapy).
- **Application to HP-Psych:** Applies. Advertising must not promise specific outcomes, cure rates, or make misleading claims about qualifications.

### Gesetz gegen unlauteren Wettbewerb (UWG)

**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/uwg_2004/](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/uwg_2004/)

- **§ 5 UWG:** Prohibition of misleading commercial practices.
- **Relevance:** "Free consultation" claims when the booking flow charges €150 may be considered misleading under § 5 UWG.

### Preisangabenverordnung (PAngV)

**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/pangv_2022/](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/pangv_2022/)

- Requires clear price indication including currency, applicable taxes, and what's included.
- **Application:** Prices on the site must clearly state EUR (not CHF), whether VAT is included (it's exempt, so state "umsatzsteuerbefreit gem. § 4 Nr. 14 UStG"), and session duration.

### Telemediengesetz (TMG) / DDG

**Note:** TMG was replaced by the Digitale-Dienste-Gesetz (DDG) effective 14 May 2024, implementing the EU Digital Services Act. Impressum requirements are now under § 5 DDG (formerly § 5 TMG).

**Source:** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/ddg/](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/ddg/)

- **§ 5 DDG:** Impressum requirements (name, address, contact, supervisory authority, professional regulations).
- **§ 6 DDG:** Special information requirements for regulated professions (must state: professional title, country of award, competent supervisory authority, applicable professional regulations).

## 6. EU — eIDAS & Electronic Signatures

### eIDAS Regulation

**Full title:** Regulation (EU) No 910/2014 on electronic identification and trust services  
**Source:** [https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2014/910/oj](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2014/910/oj)

### Signature Levels

| Level | Definition | Legal Effect | Use Case |
|-------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **SES** (Simple Electronic Signature) | Any data in electronic form attached to other data (Art. 3(10)) | Not automatically denied legal effect (Art. 25(1)) | Low-risk agreements, internal docs |
| **AES** (Advanced Electronic Signature) | Uniquely linked to signatory, capable of identifying signatory, created under signatory's sole control, linked to data in a way that detects changes (Art. 26) | Higher evidential value | Contracts, commercial agreements |
| **QES** (Qualified Electronic Signature) | AES + created by a qualified signature creation device + based on a qualified certificate (Art. 3(12)) | Equivalent to handwritten signature (Art. 25(2)) | Notarial acts, real estate, specific legal requirements |

### What Level Is Required for Therapy Intake Documents?

- **BGB (Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch) § 126a:** Elektronische Form requires QES only when the law prescribes "Schriftform" (written form).
- **Therapy contracts (Dienstvertrag, §§ 611 ff. BGB):** No Schriftform requirement. Any form of agreement is valid, including oral.
- **Informed consent:** No Schriftform requirement for psychotherapy informed consent.
- **Conclusion:** SES is legally sufficient for all therapy intake documents. AES provides better evidential value. QES is not required.

**Source (BGB § 126a):** [https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bgb/__126a.html](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bgb/__126a.html)

### DocuSign vs. OpenAPI

| | DocuSign | OpenAPI EU-SES |
|---|---------|----------------|
| **eIDAS level** | SES/AES (standard), QES (with add-on) | SES with OTP verification |
| **TSP** | DocuSign EU (eIDAS-qualified for QES) | Namirial (eIDAS-accredited, AgID-supervised) |
| **Data location** | Configurable (EU/US) | EU (Italy, pending confirmation of exact location) |
| **Cost** | €14/month flat | €0.09/signature (annual plan) |
| **AVV** | Available on request | Pending confirmation |
| **API** | REST, mature, well-documented | REST, real-time callbacks |

## 7. Austria — Practice Regulations

### Psychotherapiegesetz (PthG)

**Full title:** Bundesgesetz über die Ausübung der Psychotherapie (Psychotherapiegesetz)  
**Date:** 7 June 1990 (last amended 2024)  
**Source:** [https://www.ris.bka.gv.at/GeltendeFassung.wxe?Abfrage=Bundesnormen&Gesetzesnummer=10010620](https://www.ris.bka.gv.at/GeltendeFassung.wxe?Abfrage=Bundesnormen&Gesetzesnummer=10010620)

- **§ 1 PthG:** Psychotherapy is the scientifically grounded, comprehensive, deliberate treatment of psychosocially or psychosomatically caused behavioral disorders and states of suffering.
- **§ 3 PthG:** Practice of psychotherapy requires entry in the psychotherapist list (Psychotherapeutenliste).
- **§ 14 PthG:** Title "Psychotherapeut/in" is protected. Using it without registration is a Verwaltungsübertretung (administrative offense).
- **§ 22 PthG:** Penalties for unauthorized practice or title misuse.

**Implication:** German HP-Psych license does NOT automatically transfer. Cannot advertise as "Psychotherapeut" in Austria.

### Psychologengesetz 2013

**Source:** [https://www.ris.bka.gv.at/GeltendeFassung.wxe?Abfrage=Bundesnormen&Gesetzesnummer=20008526](https://www.ris.bka.gv.at/GeltendeFassung.wxe?Abfrage=Bundesnormen&Gesetzesnummer=20008526)

- Regulates the profession of "Psychologin/Psychologe" and "Klinische Psychologin/Klinischer Psychologe."
- Title "Psychologe/Psychologin" in Austria requires registration and recognition of qualification.
- MSc Psychology from an EU country may qualify for recognition, but requires Nostrifizierung (recognition procedure).

### Gewerbeordnung (GewO) — Lebens- und Sozialberatung

- "Lebens- und Sozialberatung" (LSB) is a regulated trade (Reglementiertes Gewerbe) under Austrian Gewerbeordnung.
- Requires Gewerbeberechtigung and proof of training (usually 584 hours + internship).
- Possible alternative for offering "Beratung" (counseling) to Austrian clients.

### Cross-Border Exception

- **EU Services Directive 2006/123/EC, Art. 16:** Freedom to provide cross-border services without local establishment. BUT Art. 17(2) excludes healthcare from automatic free movement.
- **Practical approach:** Offer "Psychoanalytische Beratung" (not psychotherapy), note that it's provided from Germany under German law, and disclaim regulated Austrian titles.

## 8. Switzerland — Practice Regulations

### Psychologieberufegesetz (PsyG)

**Full title:** Bundesgesetz über die Psychologieberufe (Psychologieberufegesetz)  
**Date:** 18 March 2011 (in force since 2013)  
**Source:** [https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/2012/679/de](https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/2012/679/de)

- **Art. 2 PsyG:** Regulates the use of professional titles and the practice of psychotherapy.
- **Art. 4 PsyG:** Title "Psychologin/Psychologe" requires a Master's degree in psychology from a recognized university and registration.
- **Art. 22 PsyG:** Psychotherapy in Switzerland requires cantonal authorization (Berufsausübungsbewilligung).
- **Art. 27 PsyG:** Title protection — "Psychotherapeut/in" is protected.

### Bilateral Agreements (CH-EU)

- **Agreement on Free Movement of Persons (FZA):** Entered into force 2002. Covers mutual recognition of professional qualifications.
- **Annex III (Mutual Recognition of Qualifications):** German qualifications may be recognized, but specific to each profession. HP-Psych is NOT a standard EU-recognized qualification (it's a German peculiarity).
- **Practical approach:** Same as Austria — offer "Beratung" from Germany, disclaim Swiss regulated titles.

### Swiss Data Protection (nDSG)

**Full title:** Bundesgesetz über den Datenschutz (DSG), revised 2020, effective 1 September 2023  
**Source:** [https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/2022/491/de](https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/2022/491/de)

- Largely GDPR-aligned but independent framework.
- Switzerland has EU adequacy decision → data transfers from EU to CH are permitted.
- Data transfers from CH to Germany are also permitted (Germany has Swiss adequacy recognition).
- No additional compliance requirements beyond what's already implemented for DSGVO.

## 9. United Kingdom — Practice Regulations

### Professional Titles

- **"Psychotherapist":** NOT a protected title in England, Wales, or Scotland.
- **"Counsellor":** NOT a protected title.
- **"Practitioner Psychologist":** PROTECTED by HCPC (Health and Care Professions Council) under the Health Professions Order 2001 (SI 2002/254).
- **"Clinical Psychologist," "Counselling Psychologist":** Protected — require HCPC registration.

**Source (HCPC protected titles):** [https://www.hcpc-uk.org/about-us/who-we-regulate/the-professions/](https://www.hcpc-uk.org/about-us/who-we-regulate/the-professions/)

### Implication
Offering "psychoanalytic counselling" or "psychoanalytic psychotherapy" is legally permitted in the UK without any registration, provided you do not use HCPC-protected titles.

### Voluntary Registration Bodies

| Body | Focus | Relevance |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **BACP** | Counselling & psychotherapy | Largest UK body, voluntary register |
| **UKCP** | Psychotherapy | Includes psychoanalytic section |
| **BPC** | Psychoanalytic psychotherapy | Specifically psychoanalytic, Freudian/Lacanian traditions |
| **HCPC** | Practitioner psychologists | Statutory regulator — protected titles only |

### UK GDPR & Data Protection Act 2018

**Source:** [https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2018/12/contents](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2018/12/contents)

- Post-Brexit, UK GDPR is a retained version of EU GDPR with UK-specific modifications.
- **EU adequacy decision for UK:** Adopted 28 June 2021, initially valid for 4 years (until June 2025). Extended/renewed — verify current status.
- **ICO (Information Commissioner's Office):** UK supervisory authority. [https://ico.org.uk/](https://ico.org.uk/)
- If adequacy lapses: need UK-specific International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or Addendum to EU SCCs.
- **Health data:** Schedule 1, Part 1 of DPA 2018 — conditions for processing special category data for healthcare.

### Electronic Signatures

- **Electronic Communications Act 2000, s.7:** Electronic signatures are admissible as evidence.
- **eIDAS does NOT apply post-Brexit.** UK has its own electronic signature framework.
- **Common law:** UK courts generally accept electronic signatures (Law Commission Report, September 2019). DocuSign widely accepted.

## 10. United States — Practice Regulations

### The Coaching vs. Therapy Distinction

**There is no federal law regulating coaching.** Coaching is regulated (or rather, NOT regulated) at the state level.

- **Psychotherapy/counseling:** Regulated by state licensing boards. Each state has its own requirements (LPC, LMHC, LCSW, LMFT, Licensed Psychologist, etc.).
- **Coaching:** Not regulated in any state as of 2026. No license required.
- **THE LINE:** Diagnosing or treating mental disorders = therapy (regulated). Personal development, self-understanding, decision-making support = coaching (unregulated).

### Key State Considerations

Some states have broader definitions of "practice of psychology" or "counseling" that could potentially encompass coaching if it resembles therapy. Key states to monitor:

| State | Risk Level | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **New York** | Medium | Broad definition of "practice of psychology" (Education Law § 7601) |
| **California** | Low-Medium | BPC § 2903 defines practice of psychology; coaching generally excluded |
| **Colorado** | Medium | Has considered (not enacted) coaching regulation |
| **Connecticut** | Low | Specifically excluded coaching from LPC definitions |
| **Minnesota** | Low | Explicitly unregulated practice model (exempt practitioners) |

### HIPAA

**Full title:** Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996  
**Source:** [https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/index.html](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/index.html)

- **Applies to:** "Covered entities" — healthcare providers who transmit health information electronically in connection with certain transactions (billing, claims).
- **Private-pay coaching:** NOT a covered entity. HIPAA does NOT apply.
- **If billing insurance:** Would become a covered entity. Avoid — stay private-pay only.
- **Best practice:** Even without HIPAA, maintain equivalent confidentiality and security standards.

### FTC Act — Advertising

- **15 U.S.C. § 45:** Prohibits "unfair or deceptive acts or practices."
- Cannot claim to treat, cure, or diagnose medical conditions without substantiation.
- Coaching testimonials must comply with FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255).
- **Source:** [https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/rules/endorsement-guides](https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/rules/endorsement-guides)

### CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)

**Source:** [https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa](https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa)

- **Applies if:** Annual revenue > $25M, OR process data of 50,000+ California consumers/households, OR derive 50% of revenue from selling personal information.
- **Likely not triggered initially** but include a preemptive CCPA notice for good practice.
- Key rights: Right to know, right to delete, right to opt out of sale.

### Tax — US-Germany Treaty

**Double Taxation Agreement (DBA):** Abkommen zwischen der Bundesrepublik Deutschland und den Vereinigten Staaten von Amerika zur Vermeidung der Doppelbesteuerung (1989, Protocol 2006).

- **Article 14 (Independent Personal Services):** Income from professional services is taxable only in the state of residence (Germany) unless the individual has a "fixed base" regularly available in the US.
- **No fixed base in US → income taxable only in Germany.**
- **W-8BEN:** File with Stripe to certify treaty benefits and avoid US withholding tax.
- **State taxes:** Some states may assert nexus based on providing services to state residents. Low risk at low volumes.

## 11. Cross-Border — EU Services & Data Transfers

### EU Services Directive (2006/123/EC)

**Source:** [https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2006/123/oj](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2006/123/oj)

- **Art. 16:** Freedom to provide cross-border services.
- **Art. 17(2):** Healthcare services are partially excluded — member states may impose requirements justified by public health.
- **Practical effect:** Can provide "counseling" (non-healthcare) freely across EU. "Psychotherapy" (healthcare) subject to host country rules.

## 12. Insurance & Liability

### Berufshaftpflichtversicherung (Professional Liability Insurance)

- **Not legally mandatory** for Heilpraktiker in Germany (unlike physicians who have mandatory insurance via their Kammer).
- **Strongly recommended.** Standard coverage: €1M–5M per claim.
- **Providers:** Hiscox, Markel, HDI — specific HP-Psych policies available.
- **International coverage:** Verify whether your policy covers clients in AT, CH, UK, USA. Many German policies have territorial limitations.
- **US exposure:** US litigation is more aggressive and awards are higher. Ensure adequate US coverage before Phase 4.

### BGB — Service Contract Liability

- **§§ 611 ff. BGB:** Psychotherapy is a Dienstvertrag (service contract), not a Werkvertrag (contract for work). The practitioner owes diligent service, not a specific outcome.
- **§ 280 BGB:** Liability for breach of duty (Pflichtverletzung). Requires fault (Verschulden, § 276 BGB).
- **§ 823 BGB:** Delictual liability (Schadensersatzpflicht) for harm caused by unlawful acts.
